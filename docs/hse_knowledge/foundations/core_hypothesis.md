## Key Propositions

### 1. Information Substrate
Space, time, and causality are **emergent** from an underlying informational layer.
- Follows Wheeler's "It from Bit" paradigm
- Every physical entity derives from binary choices
- Spacetime coordinates are **data**, not fundamental

### 2. Tachyonic Reinterpretation
Tachyons are reinterpreted as:
- **Not** faster-than-light particles (never observed)
- **Instead**: Data-carrying modes in the universal information substrate
- A "tachyonic bit" is a record specifying location/momentum in spacetime

### 3. Singularity as Interface
A micro black hole (~10²³ kg) serves as:
- **Power source**: Penrose process energy extraction (η ≤ 29.29%)
- **Information portal**: Event horizon encodes volumetric data (holographic principle)
- **Database access**: Read/write to the tachyonic substrate

### 4. Unified Singularity Interior (ER=EPR Extended)
**The radical claim:**
> All black hole singularities share ONE common interior.

- Based on Maldacena-Susskind ER=EPR conjecture
- Entangled particles connected by micro-wormholes
- Writing to one singularity affects the unified nonlocal core
- All singularity-based ships tap the **same network**

### 5. FTL as Information Transposition
Travel is not physical movement through space:
- Ship "transposes" its informational coordinates
- Destination is written to the tachyonic database
- Ship state re-instantiates at new location
- Distance becomes irrelevant—only data matters

## Critical Assessment

### Strengths
- Builds on legitimate theoretical physics (holography, ER=EPR)
- Provides coherent architecture for FTL without violating causality at the substrate level
- Unifies quantum information and gravity conceptually

### Weaknesses
- No experimental evidence for accessible tachyonic fields
- Micro black hole stabilization requires unknown physics
- Causality paradoxes may still emerge at the operational level
- Energy requirements likely astronomical

### Status: **Speculative but Coherent**

This hypothesis is a **self-consistent thought experiment** that extends real theoretical physics into visionary territory. It cannot be tested with current technology but provides a target architecture for millennium-scale research.
